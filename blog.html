Web Designer should stay up to date with technologies and trends

This is something I cannot stress enough. The number of self-acclaimed web designers who are still designing websites using old coding techniques are astonishing.

It is vital that you keep abreast of the latest trends and technologies which are emerging in your industry. If not out of passion for your work, surely out of a need to survive? Mostly you will find that the news in your industry can really be quite exciting. I'm known by my friends to be able to rattle on for hours about the latest design and marketing trends. Do they cara about what I have to say? Proudly not. But your clients will.

With the design, the generally accepted practices change frequently. Browser architecture and support is updated almost daily. As a designer, you need to be adapting to these changes.

Why you should not buy a Macbook

You may have heard that Macbooks are book that one fact that stands is that they aren't as much powerful as their rivals. Sure, it should be fine for those of you that just tweet, browse and watch but when using processor hungry applications like Photoshop, you may start noticing a decline in performance. It's powered by new Core M processor, designed specifically for thin notebookd and tablets, which isn't the best performing processor with only 1.1 - 1.3 GHz.

Take the Acer Aspire S7 for example - the S7 packs either fifth-generation Intel Dual Core, i5 or i7 processor, depending on the model you go for. With all that processing power in a 12.9mm thin machine, you'd expect a price tag to go along with it Right? Apparently not, as th S7 starts at only Ksh. 60,000. It's not the only example though, as the Toshiba Kirabook, Lenovo LaVie Z and Apple's own MacBook Air(2017 model) all come with either fifth-generation i5 or i7 processors all have similar prices to the new MacBook.
